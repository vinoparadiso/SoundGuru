# How We Align Subs & Tops for Clean, Dance‑Ready Sound

**Applies to:** Martin Audio X15 + XP118 systems  
**Why it matters:** Correct alignment = warm bass, tight kick, less fatigue, better dance floors

---

## The Short Version
When subwoofers and tops aren’t time‑aligned, bass sounds **late, hollow, or tiring**.  
We use **measurement‑based alignment** so both arrive together, giving a **full, glued, musical sound** you can dance to for hours.

This is part of our standard setup — not an optional extra.

---

## What We Use (All Industry‑Standard)
- **System processor:** Martin Audio DX0.4  
- **Measurement software:** Room EQ Wizard (REW – free, industry‑trusted)  
- **Microphone:** Boundary or measurement mic  
- **Method:** Impulse‑response time alignment

No guesswork. No “by ear only”.

---

## How the Process Works

### 1. One brain does the work
All crossover, delay and EQ happens in the **DX0.4**.
- Speaker internal crossovers are bypassed
- One crossover point, one timing reference

This avoids phase smear and keeps the system predictable.

---

### 2. Matched crossover on subs & tops
- Same crossover frequency on **both**
- Same slope type (Linkwitz‑Riley 24 dB)
- Typical range: **80–95 Hz**, depending on venue

This creates a clean hand‑off between subs and tops.

---

### 3. Time alignment using measurement
We measure:
- **Subs only**
- **One top only**

Using impulse response timing, we calculate the exact delay difference and:
- **Delay the tops** so both arrive together at the audience

Typical correction: **~2–5 ms**, depending on setup.

---

### 4. Polarity verification
We check sub polarity and keep the setting that gives:
- Stronger punch
- Better crossover summation
- Cleaner kick drum

---

### 5. Final real‑world check
We verify with music and a walk‑test:
- No “hole” in front of the rig
- Bass stays consistent as you move
- Kick feels tight, not aggressive
